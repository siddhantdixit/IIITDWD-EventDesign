Submission

Authors are invited to submit papers for presentation at the conference, describing original, previously unpublished work, not currently under review by another conference, workshop, or journal.

The authors are provided the following modes of submissions:

Online Proceeding: the paper submitted with this mode will be peer-reviewed and selected will be included in the official CEUR-WS online proceeding.

Oral Presentation: the paper submitted with this mode will be peer-reviewed, but will not be included in the official proceeding.

Guidelines for Online Proceeding

We welcome short papers (extended abstract) that present work-in-progress, preliminary or interim results. Submitted papers should not be less than 5 and not more than 6 single column pages in length (including bibliography). Authors are asked to use CEUR's LaTeX style, available here.

The authors in the paper should give meaningful affiliations. At least one of authors should have PhD and more than 4 papers indexed in DBLP (excluding ArXiv and other non peer-reviewed papers).

Guidelines for Oral Presentation

We welcome submissions for oral presentation that present work-in-progress, preliminary or interim results. Submitted papers should not be less than 2 and not more than 3 single column pages in length (excluding bibliography). Authors are asked to use the above provided CEUR's Latex style.

Students working on subjects related to the conference topics are highly encouraged to attend the ISAP and present their works

Plagiarism Policy

The paper prior to submission should be checked for plagiarism. The similarity content should not exceed 20% (in any case, either self contents or others). Further, you have to strictly implement the following ethical guidelines for publication:

1. Any form of self-plagiarism or plagiarism from others' work(s) should not be there in an article. If any model/ concept/ figure/ table/ data/ conclusive comment by any previously published work is used in your article, you should properly cite a reference to the original work.

2. The language of explaining it should not be the same as the language of the work from which you have adopted it. If you are using any copyrighted material, you should acquire prior permission from the copyright holder.

Publications

SMCA-2022 proceedings shall be submitted for online publication as a separate volume of CEUR Workshop Proceedings (ISSN 1613-0073, CEUR-WS.org). CEUR-WS Conference Proceedings have received coverage in Scopus, DBLP, and Google Scholar .
